Merlion

Singapore's iconic symbol, the Merlion, is a mythical creature with a lion's head and a fish's body. The Merlion Park statue, standing tall at 8.6 meters, embodies the nation's history. Its fish body symbolizes Singapore's humble beginnings as a fishing village, while the lion's head references its original name, "Singapura," meaning "Lion City" in Sanskrit. This legendary name came from a prince who saw a lion-like creature upon landing here. Look out for other Merlion statues as you explore Singapore, a city that continues to inspire travelers, artists, and its own people.

Garden by the Bay

Gardens by the Bay, a must-see in Singapore, offers stunning gardens, conservatories, and unique attractions like the Supertree Grove. Your Klook ticket grants access to everything, from the world's tallest indoor waterfall to displays showcasing the connection between nature and humans. With something for all ages, Gardens by the Bay guarantees a fun-filled day.

Currently, the Flower Dome features "Dahlia Dreams," a Chinese New Year display celebrating the Year of the Dragon. Admire a majestic 7-meter-tall dragon crafted from over a thousand dahlias, surrounded by symbolic lanterns and a towering dragon sculpture. This vibrant display celebrates growth and prosperity.

Singapore Zoo

Singapore Zoo followed the modern trend of displaying animals in naturalistic, ‘open’ exhibits with hidden barriers, moats, and glass between the animals and visitors. It houses the largest captive colony of orangutans in the world. There are about 315 species of animal in the zoo, of which some 16% are considered threatened species. The zoo attracts about 1.6 million visitors each year.

“Breakfast with an Orangutan” lets visitors to meet and interact closely with the orangutans in the zoo. The “Rainforest Fights Back” show is housed in the Shaw Amphitheater, the main amphitheater within the zoo. The “Elephants at Work and Play” show demonstrates how elephants are used as beasts of burden in south-east Asian countries. The “Splash Safari” show showcases the zoo’s aquatic mammals and birds. Seals and sea lions perform tricks and pelicans demonstrate how they catch fish in their beaks, while dolphin swim in the pond below. The “Animal Friends” show, housed in the Kids world amphitheatre in the zoo’s children’s section, features mostly domesticated animals such as dogs and parrots performing tricks.
